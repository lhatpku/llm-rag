# Account Types and Tax Rules in Financial Planning

## 1. Overview

Understanding the different types of investment accounts and their respective tax treatments is essential for creating an efficient financial plan. This document reviews tax-deferred, tax-free, and taxable account structures, their contribution rules, withdrawal penalties, rollover mechanics, and tax implications.

---

## 2. Employer-Sponsored Retirement Accounts

### 2.1 Traditional 401(k)

- **Tax Treatment**: Contributions are pre-tax; grows tax-deferred; withdrawals taxed as ordinary income.
- **Contribution Limit (2024)**: $23,000 + $7,500 catch-up (age 50+)
- **RMD**: Required beginning at age 73
- **Withdrawal Penalty**: 10% before age 59½ unless exception
- **Rollovers**: Can roll over into Traditional IRA or another 401(k)

### 2.2 Roth 401(k)

- **Tax Treatment**: Contributions are after-tax; qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
- **Same contribution limits as Traditional 401(k)**
- **RMD**: Required unless rolled over to Roth IRA
- **Withdrawal Rules**: Contributions accessible tax-free, earnings require age 59½ and 5-year rule

---

## 3. Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)

### 3.1 Traditional IRA

- **Tax Treatment**: Contributions may be deductible; growth is tax-deferred; withdrawals taxed as income.
- **Contribution Limit (2024)**: $7,000 + $1,000 catch-up (age 50+)
- **Deductibility**: Phased out based on income & coverage by a retirement plan at work
- **RMD**: Starts at age 73
- **Withdrawal Penalty**: 10% before 59½

### 3.2 Roth IRA

- **Tax Treatment**: Contributions not deductible; grows tax-free; qualified withdrawals tax-free
- **Same contribution limit as Traditional IRA**
- **Income Limits (2024)**: Single phase-out starts at $146,000
- **Withdrawal Rules**:
  - Contributions: Tax- and penalty-free anytime
  - Earnings: Tax-free if age 59½ and 5 years passed
- **RMD**: None during lifetime

---

## 4. Brokerage Accounts (Taxable)

- **Tax Treatment**:
  - Dividends taxed annually
  - Short-term gains taxed at ordinary income rates
  - Long-term gains taxed at capital gains rates (0%, 15%, 20%)
- **No contribution limits**
- **No withdrawal penalties**

### Use Cases:
- Flexibility
- Location for tax-efficient assets
- Source for bridging early retirement years

---

## 5. Rollovers and Transfers

- **Direct Rollover**: Trustee-to-trustee; no taxes withheld
- **Indirect Rollover**: Participant receives funds; must deposit within 60 days; 20% withheld
- **IRA to 401(k)**: Allowed by plan, but rare
- **Roth 401(k) → Roth IRA**: Popular for avoiding RMDs

### Backdoor Roth IRA:
1. Contribute to Traditional IRA (non-deductible)
2. Convert to Roth IRA
3. Subject to pro-rata rule if other IRA assets exist

---

## 6. Early Withdrawal Exceptions

Avoids 10% penalty if used for:
- First-time home purchase (IRA only)
- Higher education expenses
- Substantially equal periodic payments (SEPPs)
- Disability
- Birth/adoption expenses (up to $5,000)
